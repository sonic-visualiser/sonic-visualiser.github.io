Sonic Visualiser

A family of applications for viewing and analysing the contents of music audio recordings

Sonic Visualiser is a free, open-source application for Windows, Linux, and macOS, designed to be the first program you reach for when want to study a music recording closely. It's designed for musicologists, archivists, signal-processing researchers and anyone else looking for a friendly way to look at what lies inside the audio file.

Sonic Visualiser is one of a family of four applications:

  • Sonic Visualiser is the most general, a program for highly configurable detailed visualisation, analysis, and annotation of audio recordings.
  • Sonic Lineup is for quick time-aligned comparative visualisation of groups of related audio files, such as different performances of the same score, or different takes of an instrumental part.
  • Tony is for close analysis and interactive annotation of pitch in unaccompanied singing.
  • Sonic Annotator is a non-interactive command-line application for batch audio feature extraction, using some of the same methods as are available in Sonic Visualiser.
